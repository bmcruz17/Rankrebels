The build

The TARO ecosystem, in five parts

One custom platform — your branding, your workflows — that replaces the patchwork. Built around how TARO actually delivers fiber.

01 · The field + corporate app

iPhone & Android, one team

Field reps, crews, subcontractors, and corporate officers in a single app — so the job in the field and the job in the office are finally the same job.

GPS-stamped captures & redlines

Daily reports, photos, and as-built redlines tied to the GIS design — online or offline in the field, synced the moment there's signal.

Dispatch & work orders

Assign crews and subs, push the route and scope to their phone, and watch status move from assigned → in progress → QA → done.

Timesheets & production

Crews log hours and units in the field; payroll and billing read the same numbers — no double entry.

Design → field → invoice

The model the best fiber contractors are moving to: one thread from the GIS plan to the as-built to the invoice. No re-keying between five systems.

04 · The Board Room

AI advisors on call — like a board that never sleeps

Functioning board members, powered by Claude, that know TARO's numbers and context. Ask a question in plain English and get a straight answer — on operations, finance, business development, or compliance.

Operations

Crew allocation, schedule risk, and where a route is about to slip.

"Crew 3 is over-committed next week — move the Carmel make-ready to Crew 5."

Finance

Margin by project, cash flow, and what's dragging your billing cycle.

"Two of your three lowest-margin jobs share the same sub — worth a rate talk."

Business Dev

Which certifications and primes to pursue, and where the next contract is.

"You're GSA-listed but not 8(a) — that gap is worth pursuing for federal set-asides."

Compliance

Renewals, prevailing-wage, and certification eligibility, watched for you.

"Your MBE recert window opens in 60 days — here's the document checklist."

Advisors see your live dashboards, so the advice is about your jobs and numbers — not generic tips.

05 · Outreach & growth engine

Turn certifications into contracts

MBE, NMSDC, CPUC, and a fresh GSA Schedule get TARO seen — they don't hand you the work. This engine runs the "land and expand" play: surface the right opportunities and primes, then help you pursue them.

Opportunity radar

RFPs, prime-contractor needs, and utility/government solicitations matched to TARO's NAICS codes and service lines.

Prime & partner pipeline

Track relationships with carriers and primes (Dycom, Comcast, Astound…) and the corporate supplier-diversity programs that spend with MBEs.

Proposal & capability statements

Generate tailored capability statements and proposal drafts from TARO's record — so responding is hours, not days.

Close the cert gap

A roadmap for set-aside certifications you don't hold yet (8(a), HUBZone) that open federal doors.
